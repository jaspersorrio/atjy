SOCIAL CAUSE

Education Opportunites for Kids in Poverty

We live in a society where everything just progresses so quickly that we tend to become oblivious to our own surroundings. While it is true that the world is doing very well in terms of economic and technological advancements, what many people do not realise is that, poverty still exist amongst us today. Poverty is nothing but a thief that steals away all the good things that a person deserves to have such as a life filled with dignity and opportunities. Often, the most innocent victims are the kids.

Unlike the kids in Singapore who are able to receive a well-rounded education, many of the kids whom I met during my voluntary trip to the rural part of Chiang Mai, do not even have access to basic education. They have to live in an unfavourable environment with poor sanitation and many of them also have lack of access to clean water which can be detrimental to their development.

In the past, I still remember how I would often complain about the life I had in Singapore. But as I saw the situation that the kids in Chiang Mai were facing, I began to realise how blessed I actually am to be able to live in such a wonderful country with a comfortable life.

Many of the kids there were so hungry for knowledge that they were even willing to travel a long distance from home just so they can attend a lesson conducted by us. It was then that I started to realise that to the kids there, the opportunity to study is something that is so precious to them. Yet, many of us in Singapore would often complain about having to go to school.

Ever since then, I began to cherish the education opportunity given to me even more than before. Much more than that, I was also very moved to help these kids out of the situation they are in. As I graduate, I hope to be able to work with non-profit organisations such as World Vision Singapore to find ways to enable these kids to have access to at least a basic education.

Only education can break the vicious cycle of poverty that they are in right now. The lives of many generations to come can be changed completely by a single step that we decide to take today.
